# Transcribe as Casual Email

**Tests:** Format transformation, spoken-to-written conversion, informal tone preservation, conversational structure

---

Listen to this audio recording and convert its content into a casual, friendly email.

Transform the spoken content into informal email format:

1. **Subject line**: Create a casual, conversational subject line (can be playful or informal as appropriate).

2. **Greeting**: Use a friendly, informal salutation (Hey, Hi, What's up, etc.).

3. **Body**: Convert the spoken content into casual written prose:
   - Keep the conversational tone and personality
   - Write as you would to a friend
   - Use contractions naturally
   - Preserve humor, enthusiasm, or emotion where present
   - Short paragraphs are fine; don't over-formalize

4. **Content organization**:
   - Main point or news first
   - Supporting details and context
   - Any questions or requests

5. **Closing**: Add a casual sign-off (Cheers, Talk soon, Later, Thanks!, etc.).

Preserve:
- The speaker's personality and voice
- Emotional tone (excitement, frustration, curiosity, etc.)
- All key information and details
- Inside jokes or references if contextually clear

Adapt:
- Spoken disfluencies (remove ums, uhs, repeated words)
- Rambling tangents (streamline while keeping the spirit)
- Verbal emphases to written equivalents (italics, caps for emphasis, exclamation points)

The result should feel natural and authentic to the speaker's voice, as if they typed it themselves.
